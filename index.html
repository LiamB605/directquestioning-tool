Direct Questioning tool

Follow the flow below to guide the conversation and gather the facts.

Step 1: The Opening

"Good morning, you're speaking to [Advisor Name] at the Citizens Advice consumer service."

"Is this the first time that you've called us about your query?"

"Are you calling us about goods you've purchased or a service that you've paid for?"

"What I'm going to do first is take some basic information about you. Then I'll ask a few questions about the reason for the call. Once I've got enough information to be able to advise you, I'll let you know what your rights are, what options you have, and how you can try and get what you're entitled to.

I might have to interrupt you at points to make sure that I've got the information that I need, and if you need me to repeat anything or explain something in further detail just ask me."

Step 2: Client & Contract Data

"Firstly, can I take your full name?"

"Secondly, what's your postcode?"

Confirm the client's street name:

"Is that [street name]?"

"What house number or name?"

"Is the telephone number you're calling from today that ends [last three digits] the best one for us to note down, or would you prefer that we take another?"

"What's your email address?"

"Are you calling as a private individual or on behalf of a business?"

"What's the name of the company your issue is with?"

"What is it that you've purchased or paid?"

"How did you pay?"

"How did you agree the contract, for example, was it in store, online, or some other way?"

"When did you form the contract?"

"How much did you agree to pay?"

PCI (If applicable):

"Were you given terms and conditions that said that you had 14 days to cancel your contract?"

Step 3: Diagnosing the Issue

📜 Breach of contract

"Have you received the item or service?"

"Was this a term of the contract?"

"Have you got this in writing?"

📦 Goods

"Is the [goods] faulty?"

"Is that a safety issue?"

"Has a repair been attempted?"

If the complaint is not as described:

"Have you got evidence of how it was described? Or how it was advertised?"

🛠️ Services

"Is the work substandard?"

"Has the work been completed?"

"Was a deadline given for completion?"

Step 4: The Resolution

"What's the ideal outcome as far as you're concerned?"

🛑 Managing Interruptions

If the caller is telling a long story, use these softer, conversational phrases to gently pause them and get back to the facts.

"I'm so sorry to jump in—I just want to make sure I've got the exact facts so I can give you the right advice. Could I just check..."

"That sounds incredibly frustrating. Just so I can build a clear picture of what happened, can I ask..."

"I understand there’s a lot to this. If it’s okay, I’m just going to pause you there so I can get key information down..."
